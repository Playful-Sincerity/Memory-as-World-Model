# Transformer Architecture as AM Validation

From play session 2026-04-09. How transformers do what AM proposes to do.

## The Parallel
- **Weights = Matrix (AM's subconscious graph)** — memories stored in weight nodes, not as files. Compressed knowledge that gets decompressed on traversal.
- **Attention = Spreading activation** — selecting which part of the weight graph to infer on. Multiple attention heads = multiple parallel traversals combined.
- **Context window = Trees (AM's working memory)** — the active, focused subset currently being processed.
- **Extended thinking = Mirror (AM's consciousness)** — the system reflecting on its own processing.

## Key Insight: Depth of Decompression
When weights bring you to the "area" of a concept, how deep you decompress depends on confidence/curiosity/relevance. This maps directly to AM's care-budgeted depth in spreading activation. High care = deep decompression = more detail. Low care = surface-level = compressed summary.

## Attention Head Interference = Resonance
Multiple heads can constructively or destructively interfere. Constructive = reinforced signal (high confidence). Destructive = suppressed noise. This is literally resonance — and may be how AM should implement multi-path activation.

## Why AM Is Still Needed
Transformers hallucinate because attention connects tokens with high similarity in weight-space even when they shouldn't be connected in reality. AM's explicit graph prevents this — edges are there or they aren't. The graph IS ground truth. This validates AM's core thesis: the graph should be the interpretable world model, not the weights.
