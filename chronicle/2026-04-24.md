## 17:00 — [Research]

**What:** Captured Wisdom's articulation of the **unified multimodal substrate** as a major research direction for MWM. New design doc at `design/multimodal-unified-substrate.md` (full articulation), plus elevated treatment in the paper — Section 3.1 node description now names the "readable text layer is canonical; modality-specific content rides on it" commitment, and Section 8's multimodal entry expands from one sentence to a full paragraph articulating why it's a load-bearing research direction (not a minor implementation question).
**Why:** Wisdom: "we should basically be creating a unified file structure that can store text image understanding data... the full multimodal existence in a single file structure that is interpretable... where an image memory as you would a textual memory... it's all able to be seen and viewed and read. By the model and by humans." The key insight: if MWM's interpretability promise depends on the substrate being readable, and non-text memories live in opaque shared-latent embeddings, then interpretability ends at the modality boundary. The unified-legible-substrate commitment is what preserves the promise across the full multimodal life of the agent.
**Means:** This extends the interpretability-by-design claim to its hardest case. It also reframes multimodality in the paper from a minor open question to a named research frontier. The substrate sketch: every node has a natural-language description as its canonical readable layer; modality-specific data (pixels, waveforms) are pointers + embeddings hanging off that layer. Imagination and dreams require this natively because recombinative trees cross modality boundaries. Likely Round 7 research territory.
