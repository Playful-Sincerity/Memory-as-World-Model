**What:** Wisdom surfaced a major cross-project coupling: ULP is not just an efficiency layer for LLMs; it is the candidate **unified semantic substrate** for MWM's nodes. The reframing flips ULP's primary research target from "make LLMs more efficient" (where the consumer substrate stays black-box and gains are diminishing) to "be the readable substrate MWM nodes are encoded in" (where compositional gains are legible at every step and validation is structural rather than benchmarked-against-frontier-models). Captured the insight in five places: (1) added a new §9.4 "A candidate substrate: ULP" to the MWM paper introducing ULP as the specific candidate answer to the §9 unified-substrate question, with explicit treatment of three load-bearing properties (modality universality without latent opacity, storage compactness, interpretability-profile shift) and the honest open question (translation-vs-native-read at the LLM rendering layer); (2) added a forward-reference in §10's Multimodal substrate details entry; (3) created `MWM/connections/ulp.md` and `ULP/connections/mwm.md` as the bidirectional connection record; (4) created `MWM/ideas/2026-04-25-ulp-as-mwm-substrate.md` capturing the full insight with concrete next moves; (5) updated both memory files (`project_mwm.md`, `project_ulp.md`) to flag the coupling. Rebuilt PDF; 24 pp, 153 KB, compiles clean. Also added a new bullet to `wisdom-profile/intellectual-signature.md` capturing the portfolio-level reframing move (diminishing-returns analysis applied to his own projects' positioning).

**Why:** Wisdom verbatim: *"I thought for a long time that ULP will make language models drastically more efficient. And I think that's true. But I actually wonder, I think maybe the gains are actually kind of diminishing in comparison to the gains that would be allowed as an optimization system for MWM. ... that universal substrate is ULP. It's the binary semantic run-lengths that can store information in the very most efficient possible way. ... Making me want to focus on ULP pretty heavily these days."* The MWM paper's §9 just committed to a unified multimodal substrate and acknowledged it as a research program with no specific candidate. ULP is exactly the candidate that satisfies §9.1's commitment without forcing the lossy text-as-canonical-layer fallback. ULP also partially answers Eric's storage critique — binary RLE is dramatically more compact than per-node natural-language descriptions, shifting the per-agent storage frontier from terabyte-territory (at $N \to 10^9$) back toward gigabyte-territory at the same node count. The coupling is staged carefully: near-term MWM prototype stays text-only as the paper commits; ULP-as-substrate becomes MWM's Round 7 research direction; both projects continue on their own implementation timelines until ready to integrate.

**Means:** This reframes ULP's primary research target. The frontier-LLM-efficiency framing has been ULP's pitch for years (33 ChatGPT conversations, ~300K words of development history). Replacing it with the substrate-for-MWM framing gives ULP a research target with cleaner success metrics (does the encoding preserve cross-modal semantics with bounded loss? — a structural test) and a research target that doesn't depend on beating moving frontier benchmarks in a fast-moving field. For MWM, §9 stops being an open philosophical question and becomes a research program with a specific experimental direction: does ULP-as-canonical-layer hold the §9 commitments better than text-as-canonical-layer does? For SSP — as MWM's parent — this propagates substrate-level interpretability throughout all nine SSP subsystems, since they all consume MWM's graph. Both projects remain concept-stage; coupling timelines and risks. The discipline is to stage the coupling, not rush it. Operational next moves: when ULP work next picks up, redirect part of the focus toward the substrate question; expand `design/multimodal-unified-substrate.md` with a section developing the ULP-as-substrate hypothesis; the text-only MWM prototype proceeds in parallel without dependence on ULP.
